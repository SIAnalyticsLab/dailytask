🌍 SI Analytics 30-Day Data Visualization Challenge — Day 3

Pediatric HIV Survey — Yaoundé, Cameroon (2021)
📁 Dataset: yaounde_ped_hiv_survey.csv

🧩 Background

Today’s dataset comes from a pediatric HIV care survey conducted at a hospital in Yaoundé, Cameroon, between July and August 2021. It includes information from children aged 0–5 years who attended the HIV care unit during routine visits.

The survey recorded each child’s sex, health area, disease symptoms, feeding mode, vitamin A supplementation, ARV regimen, and water container hygiene frequency.

📄 Dataset name: yaounde_ped_hiv_survey.csv

Key variables:
VariableDescription
order_numberUnique record ID
sexChild’s gender (m/f)
health_area_nameHealth area within Yaoundé
zone_numNumeric zone identifier
visit_day / visit_monthDate of visit
disease_past_monthDisease(s) reported in the past month
vitamin_a_receivedYes/No
feeding_modeExclusive, Mixed, or Other
water_container_washing_freqe.g., “Before each refill”, “Never”
arv_treatmentAntiretroviral regimen (e.g., TDF+3TC+EFV)

🎯 Daily Task Objective

Build a simple health insights dashboard showing patterns of common childhood illnesses and care practices among HIV-exposed children.

💡 Core Question: “What are the most common illnesses and care patterns among children receiving pediatric HIV care in Yaoundé?”

🧮 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Load and Explore the Dataset
    • R: read_csv("yaounde_ped_hiv_survey.csv")
    • Python: pd.read_csv("yaounde_ped_hiv_survey.csv")
    • Power BI: Home → Get Data → Text/CSV → Select the file
    Inspect the dataset and check for missing values or inconsistencies (e.g., disease name capitalization).
  2. Prepare and Summarize the Data
    Create summaries to capture:
    • Frequency of diseases reported (disease_past_month)
    • Proportion of children who received Vitamin A
    • Distribution of feeding modes
    • Distribution of ARV regimens
    • Water hygiene practices across health areas
    💡 Optional: Group results by health_area_name to explore local differences.
  3. Build Your Dashboard
    Use your preferred tool (R, Python, or Power BI) to visualize:
    • 📊 Bar chart: Top 5 diseases reported
    • 🧮 Donut chart or KPI card: % of children who received Vitamin A
    • 🍽️ Pie chart: Distribution of feeding modes
    • 💊 Stacked bar chart: ARV regimen distribution by sex or health area
    • 💧 Horizontal bar chart: Water container washing frequency
    💡 Include clear titles, consistent colors, and readable labels.
  4. Add Key Insight Cards (Optional)
    Use value boxes or summary text to display:
    • Total number of children surveyed
    • % receiving Vitamin A
    • Most frequent disease
