📊 SI Analytics 30-Day Data Visualization Challenge — Daily Task 2

🧩 Background

You’ve received a sample Sales Orders Dataset ( Sales_Orders.xlsx), representing a tech retail and wholesale company selling products such as robot kits, drone kits, eBooks, blueprints, and training videos across multiple U.S. states.

Each record includes:
• Order details: OrderNum, OrderDate, OrderType, CustomerType, CustName, CustState
• Product details: ProdCategory, ProdNumber, ProdName
• Sales metrics: Quantity, Price, Discount, and Order Total

Your goal is to explore this data, derive insights, and build an engaging dashboard using R, Python, or Power BI.

🎯 Daily Task Objective

Create and share one visual dashboard that answers this core question:
💡 “What are the top-performing product categories and customer types driving sales performance?”

🧮 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Load and Explore the Data
    • R: read.csv() or readr::read_csv()
    • Python: pd.read_csv()
    • Power BI: Home → Get Data → Text/CSV
    Inspect the fields, handle any currency symbols, and ensure numeric columns are correctly formatted.
  2. Calculate Key Metrics
    Create summary measures such as:
    • Total Sales = sum of Order Total
    • Average Discount per category or customer type
    • Total Quantity Sold
  3. Build Your Dashboard
    Use any of the three tools to visualize:
    • 🧱 Total Sales by Product Category (bar or column chart)
    • 🧭 Sales by Customer Type (Business vs Individual)
    • 📆 Sales Trend by Order Date
    • 🗺️ Sales Distribution by State
    💡 Tip: Include value boxes or KPI cards for “Total Sales” and “Total Orders.”
  4. Add Basic Design Features
    • Apply your preferred color palette and clean layout.
    • Label axes clearly and give each visual a meaningful title.
    • In R/Python: render as a Quarto dashboard.
    • In Power BI: use a single-page report layout.
