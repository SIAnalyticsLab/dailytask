💵 SI Analytics 30-Day Data Visualization Challenge — Day 6

U.S. State Government Finances (1992–2019)

🧩 Background

Today’s dataset comes from the CORGIS Dataset Project, built using the Annual Survey of State Government Finances by the U.S. Census Bureau. It summarizes revenues, expenditures, debts, and assets for each U.S. state from 1992 to 2019, providing a rich longitudinal view of fiscal health and spending priorities.

📄 Dataset name: finance.csv
📍 Source: CORGIS Finance Dataset
📈 Official Survey Reference: U.S. Census – State Government Finances

🔑 Key Variables

VariableDescription
StateFull state name (e.g., “ALABAMA”)
YearReporting year (1992–2019)
Totals.RevenueTotal money received by the state from all external sources
Totals.ExpenditureTotal money spent by the state government
Totals.TaxTotal tax revenue
Totals.IntergovernmentalRevenue received from other levels of government
Details.Education.Education TotalSpending on education (K–12, colleges, etc.)
Details.Health.Health Total ExpenditureSpending on health services and programs
Details.Transportation.Highways.Highways Total ExpenditureSpending on highway infrastructure
Details.Welfare.Welfare Institution Total ExpenditureSpending on welfare programs
Totals.Debt at end of fiscal yearOutstanding government debt at fiscal year-end

🎯 Daily Task Objective

Create a U.S. State Finance Dashboard to explore and compare how states allocate funds and generate revenue over time.

💡 Core Question:
“How do government revenues, expenditures, and debts vary across states and years — and which sectors receive the largest investments?”

🧮 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Load and Explore the Data
    • R: read_csv("finance.csv")
    • Python: pd.read_csv("finance.csv")
    • Power BI: Home → Get Data → Text/CSV → Select file or paste link
    Inspect data dimensions and ensure numerical columns (e.g., Totals.Revenue, Totals.Expenditure) are properly formatted as numeric values.
  2. Clean and Prepare the Data
    • Filter for a specific year (e.g., 2019) for static analysis, or use all years for trend analysis.
    • Calculate new indicators such as:
    • 💰 Budget Balance = Totals.Revenue – Totals.Expenditure
    • 📊 Debt Ratio = (Debt at end of fiscal year ÷ Totals.Revenue) × 100
    • 🏫 Education Share = Education Total ÷ Totals.Expenditure × 100
  3. Build Your Dashboard
    Use R (ggplot2/plotly/quarto), Python (matplotlib/plotly/dash), or Power BI to visualize the following:
    • 🗺️ Map or Bar Chart: Top 10 states by total expenditure or debt (most recent year)
    • 📈 Line Chart: Revenue vs Expenditure trend (1992–2019) for a selected state
    • 📊 Stacked Bar Chart: Breakdown of spending by sector (Education, Health, Welfare, Transportation)
    • 💸 Scatter Plot: Relationship between revenue and debt across states
    • 🧮 KPI Cards:
      • Average Revenue
      • Average Expenditure
      • Average Debt Ratio
    💡 Power BI Tip: Use the “State” field as a slicer to switch between states interactively.
    💡 R/Python Tip: Facet or animate charts by year to show fiscal trends.
  4. Highlight Key Insights
    In a text box or caption, summarize findings such as:
    • States with the highest education or health expenditure
    • How tax revenue compares to total revenue
    • Debt trends over time for selected states
