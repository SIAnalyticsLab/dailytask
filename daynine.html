✈️ SI Analytics 30-Day Data Visualization Challenge — Day 9

Flight Reservation Dataset: Airline Pricing & Travel Time Dashboard
📁 Dataset: flights.csv

🧩 Background

Today’s dataset — “flights.csv” — contains flight information from multiple airlines and routes across India. It includes details such as airline name, travel date, source and destination cities, departure time, duration, total stops, and ticket price.

This dataset provides an excellent opportunity to analyze airline pricing trends, travel durations, and service differences — key for business intelligence, travel analytics, and operations optimization.

📄 Dataset name: flights.csv
📍 Source: SI Analytics GitHub Repository

🔑 Key Variables

VariableDescription
AirlineName of the airline (e.g., Air India, IndiGo, Jet Airways)
Date_of_JourneyFlight date
SourceDeparture city
DestinationArrival city
Dep_TimeScheduled departure time
DurationTotal flight duration (e.g., 2h 50m)
Total_StopsNumber of stops (e.g., 1 stop, non-stop)
Additional_InfoLayover or flight info (e.g., 1 Long layover)
PriceFlight ticket price (in INR)

🎯 Daily Task Objective

Build a Flight Reservation Dashboard that answers:

💡 Core Question: “How do flight prices and durations vary across airlines, routes, and total stops?”

Your goal is to visualize patterns that help travelers or analysts identify the best flight deals and performance differences between airlines.

🧮 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Load and Explore the Data
    • R: read_csv("flights.csv")
    • Python: pd.read_csv("flights.csv")
    • Power BI: Home → Get Data → Text/CSV → Select the file or paste the GitHub link
    Inspect columns such as Airline, Source, Destination, and Price. Clean text fields (remove extra spaces, standardize airline names). Convert Duration to total minutes for analysis.
  2. Clean and Prepare
    • Extract hour or day from Dep_Time for temporal insights.
    • Create derived fields:
    • ⏱️ Duration (minutes) – convert “2h 30m” to numeric
    • 💰 Average Price per Airline – mean of Price by Airline
    • ✈️ Flights per Route – count of flights by Source–Destination
    • Handle “non-stop” and “1 stop” consistently for comparisons.
  3. Build Your Dashboard
    Use R, Python, or Power BI to create visuals such as:
    • ✈️ Bar Chart: Average flight price by airline
    • 🕒 Column Chart: Average duration by airline or total stops
    • 🌆 Matrix/Map: Route frequency (Source → Destination)
    • 💰 Scatter Plot: Price vs Duration (colored by airline)
    • 📊 Donut Chart: Flight distribution by number of stops
    💡 Power BI Tip: Add filters for “Airline” and “Source City.”
    💡 R/Python Tip: Use color palettes (teal → gold → orange) for clarity and consistency.
  4. Add Summary KPI Cards
    Include:
    • 💸 Average Flight Price
    • 🕓 Average Duration (hours)
    • ✈️ Total Number of Flights
    • 🛫 % of Non-stop Flights
