🏃‍♀️ SI Analytics 30-Day Data Visualization Challenge — Day 8

Physical Activity and Sedentary Behavior Survey
📁 Dataset: physical_activity.csv

🧩 Background

Today’s dataset — “physical_activity.csv” — captures daily physical activity and sedentary behavior patterns from adults and children in different households. The data comes from an observational study designed to understand how lifestyle behaviors (sitting time, light activity, and moderate-to-vigorous activity) relate to Body Mass Index (BMI) and adherence to World Health Organization (WHO) physical activity recommendations.

📄 Dataset name: physical_activity.csv
📍 Source: SI Analytics GitHub Repository

🔑 Key Variables

VariableDescription
personal_id, household_idParticipant and household identifiers
sexGender (M = Male, F = Female)
statusAge group (Adulte or Enfant)
bmiBody Mass Index (kg/m²)
sedentary_ap_s_dayAverage sedentary activity per day (seconds/day)
light_ap_s_dayAverage light activity per day (seconds/day)
mvpa_s_dayModerate-to-vigorous physical activity (seconds/day)
oms_recommendationWhether participant meets WHO physical activity recommendation (Yes/No)

🎯 Daily Task Objective

Build a Physical Activity Dashboard that answers:

💡 Core Question: “How do daily activity patterns differ by age group, sex, and BMI — and who meets WHO physical activity targets?”

You will visualize and interpret trends linking sedentary time, active minutes, and BMI to highlight health behavior differences.

🧮 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Load and Explore the Dataset
    • R: read_csv("physical_activity.csv")
    • Python: pd.read_csv("physical_activity.csv")
    • Power BI: Home → Get Data → Text/CSV → Select the file
    Inspect missing values (e.g., bmi, mvpa_s_day) and remove or impute as needed for clarity.
  2. Prepare and Summarize the Data
    • Convert seconds/day to minutes/day:
    • mvpa_min_day = mvpa_s_day / 60
    • sedentary_min_day = sedentary_ap_s_day / 60
    • Group data by:
    • Status (Adult vs Enfant)
    • Sex (M/F)
    • OMS recommendation (Yes/No)
    • Calculate:
    • Average BMI, sedentary time, and MVPA minutes/day for each group.
    • % meeting WHO recommendations by sex or age group.
  3. Build Your Dashboard
    Use R, Python, or Power BI to visualize your findings:
    • 📊 Bar Chart: Average MVPA (minutes/day) by age group and sex
    • 🧘 Stacked Bar Chart: Proportion meeting WHO recommendations (Yes/No) by age group
    • 🕒 Column Chart: Sedentary vs. Light activity (minutes/day) by group
    • ⚖️ Scatter Plot: BMI vs. MVPA (colored by WHO recommendation status)
    • 📈 Box Plot: Distribution of MVPA or Sedentary time across sexes
    💡 Power BI Tip: Add data labels and slicers for “sex” and “status.”
    💡 R/Python Tip: Use color gradients (e.g., teal → orange) to highlight “Yes” vs “No” for WHO adherence.
  4. Add Key Insight Cards
    Include a few high-level metrics:
    • 🧍 Average BMI of adults and children
    • ⏱️ Average sedentary time (hours/day)
    • 🏃 % of participants meeting WHO recommendation
    • 👩‍👧 Number of children vs adults surveyed
